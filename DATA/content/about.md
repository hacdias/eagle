Hi, I'm Henrique (he/him)! 👋

I'm from a little a town in Portugal 🇵🇹 and I'm now doing my Master's degree 🎓 in Computer Science and Engineering at [TU/e][tue], in Eindhoven 🇳🇱. At the same time, I'm working at [Protocol Labs](https://protocol.ai) on the decentralization of the web.

I believe in the transparency and openness of the world, in a united Europe 🇪🇺 and that all citizens must have equal rights 🏳️‍🌈. There are a lot of problems in the world and we all are part of them and should work towards correcting them, making Earth a better place for all of us to live.

Back in 2014, I wrote my first ever post in this website, in Portuguese, where I said my life goal as a Software Engineer was to [improve the world](/articles/2014/07/apresentacao/) somehow. Even though that is a cliché, it doesn't mean it is not true. So, here  are some interesting problems I think solving would be a great step on improving the web we have nowadays, hence improving the world:

1. **Decentralization** of the web in general, in which all of us become a functional part of the web instead of just a passive individual, liberating ourselves from silos, and having power over our own data.
2. **Banking** through blockchain. Instead of relying on institutions, use blockchain technology for verifiable, autonomous, anonymous banking. Smart contracts, for example, can be used as inviolable contracts to ensure trust.

## 📰 A bit of history

In **2013**, I started working as an Editor on [Pplware][pplware], which is a Portuguese project that delivers the most recent news about technology to the Portuguese speaking community. It has a blog, a forum, a "sell your used stuff" kind of platform and many discussion groups. [Read more →][pplware]

In late **2016**, I joined a project called [Upframe][upframe] as Head of Technology in which I had the opportunity to talk and to work with people from different educational backgrounds, such as design and economics, to name a few. The goal was to boost the growth of the Portuguese technology panorama. [Read more →][upframe]

In late **2017**, I was offered an Open Source Scholarship at [Protocol Labs][pl], which is the creator of [IPFS][ipfs] - the InterPlanetary File System, a decentralized and peer-to-peer protocol. In the beginning, my focus relies mostly on IPFS Desktop and WebUI which are graphical interfaces to manage a node. [Read more →][pl]

In the meanwhile, I was promoted to Software Engineer at Protocol Labs and my focus has shifted to [Testground][testground], a testing, benchmarking and simulating platform for decentralized systems.

In June **2020** I finished my Bachelor's degree in Information Systems and Computer Engineering at [Técnico][ist], Lisboa ([certificate][istcert]). Then, I moved to Eindhoven, in the Netherlands, to start my Master's degree in Computer Science and Engineering at [TU/e][tue].

Take a look at my [projects](/projects/) page and at my [GitHub profile][gh] to know more about what I've been working on recently. In addition, you can support my work, [buying me a coffee][bmc]!

It was a mere coincidence between the first letters of my first name and middle names - 'hac' - and last name - 'dias' - that I happen to enjoy.
